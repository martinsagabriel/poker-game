Poker Hand Rankings & Rules

Texas Hold'em basics:

Royal Flush

This hand contains five cards in sequence, all of the same suit, ending in Ace.

Straight Flush

This hand contains five cards in sequence, all of the same suit.

Four of a Kind

This hand contains all four cards of one rank and any other unmatched card (kicker).

Full House

This hand contains three matching cards of one rank and two matching cards of another rank.

Flush

This hand contains all five cards of the same suit, but not in sequence.

Straight

This hand contains five cards of sequential rank in at least two different suits.

Three of a Kind

This hand contains three cards of the same rank, plus two cards which are not of this rank nor the same as each other (kickers).

Two Pair

This hand contains two cards of one rank, plus two cards of another rank, plus one kicker.

One Pair

This hand contains two cards of one rank, plus three cards which are not of this rank nor the same as each other (kickers).

High Card

Made of any five cards not meeting any of the above requirements. The highest card determines the hand's value.

Game Flow

  1. Blinds: Two players post forced bets (Small Blind and Big Blind).
  2. Preflop: Each player receives two private cards ("hole cards"). Betting round begins.
  3. Flop: Three community cards are dealt face up. Another betting round occurs.
  4. Turn: A fourth community card is dealt. Another betting round.
  5. River: A fifth and final community card is dealt. Final betting round.
  6. Showdown: If more than one player remains, hands are revealed. The player with the best five-card poker hand (using any combination of their two hole cards and the five community cards) wins the pot.

Player Actions

  • Fold: Discard hand and forfeit any chance to win the current pot.
  • Check: Pass the action without betting (only possible if no bet has been made in the current round).
  • Bet: Make the first bet in a betting round.
  • Call: Match the amount of the current bet or raise.
  • Raise: Increase the amount of the current bet.

The goal is to win chips by either having the best hand at showdown or by forcing all other players to fold.

Classificação de Mãos e Regras do Poker

Princípios básicos do Texas Hold'em:

Fluxo do Jogo

  1. Blinds: Dois jogadores fazem apostas obrigatórias (Small Blind e Big Blind).
  2. Preflop: Cada jogador recebe duas cartas privadas ("hole cards"). A rodada de apostas começa.
  3. Flop: Três cartas comunitárias são viradas para cima. Outra rodada de apostas ocorre.
  4. Turn: Uma quarta carta comunitária é virada. Outra rodada de apostas.
  5. River: Uma quinta e última carta comunitária é virada. Última rodada de apostas.
  6. Showdown: Se mais de um jogador permanecer, as mãos são reveladas. O jogador com a melhor mão de poker de cinco cartas (usando qualquer combinação de suas duas cartas privadas e as cinco cartas comunitárias) ganha o pote.

Ações do Jogador

  • Fold (Desistir): Descartar a mão e perder qualquer chance de ganhar o pote atual.
  • Check (Passar): Passar a ação sem apostar (só é possível se nenhuma aposta foi feita na rodada atual).
  • Bet (Apostar): Fazer a primeira aposta em uma rodada de apostas.
  • Call (Pagar): Igualar o valor da aposta ou aumento atual.
  • Raise (Aumentar): Aumentar o valor da aposta atual.

O objetivo é ganhar fichas, seja tendo a melhor mão no showdown ou forçando todos os outros jogadores a desistirem.
